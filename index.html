Research Themes

Three questions motivate the group's work.

From interglacial coastlines to Paleozoic and Precambrian strata, the group's research links field observations to quantitative questions about sea level, stratigraphic correlation, and geochemical change.

Sea level and ice sheets

How do past shorelines constrain ice-sheet change?

Work in the Bahamas, Barbados, northwest Europe, and beyond uses stratigraphy, geochronology, and geophysics to reconstruct interglacial sea level and test ice-sheet behavior.

Stratigraphy and sedimentary signals

When is a recorded signal global, and when is it local?

The group studies how transport, facies architecture, sequence stratigraphy, and age uncertainty shape the proxy records preserved in sedimentary rocks.

Ancient carbon cycle and diagenesis

What do carbonate geochemical records really mean?

Carbonate geochemistry can illuminate major environmental transitions, but only when local overprints and diagenetic effects are treated as part of the science, not noise to ignore.

How We Work

Modern quantitative tools, anchored by field geology.

Detailed measured sections, drone imagery, GPS, and sedimentologic context are paired with Bayesian inference, geochemical analysis, machine learning, and numerical modeling. Field campaigns generate the observations and samples that anchor later interpretation and modeling.
